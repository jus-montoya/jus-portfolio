Oil Changes

Changing your car’s oil regularly keeps the engine lubricated and running smoothly.

Most vehicles need an oil change every 8,000 to 12,000 kilometers, depending on the type of oil used.

Neglecting oil changes can lead to engine damage and costly repairs.

Recommended: Every 6 months to 1 year or 8,000 km

Use the correct oil grade specified in your owner’s manual.

Signs you need an oil change include dark, gritty oil or engine noise.

  • Check oil level monthly
  • Use synthetic oil for longer intervals
  • Replace oil filter with each change
  • Warm engine before draining oil
  • Dispose of used oil responsibly
Check oil: Pull dipstick → Wipe → Reinsert → Read level
Tire Care

Proper tire maintenance improves safety, fuel efficiency, and ride comfort.

Check tire pressure monthly and before long trips.

Rotate tires every 8,000 to 13,000 kilometers to ensure even wear.

Recommended Pressure: Found on driver’s door sticker (in kPa)

Inspect tread depth using a coin or tread gauge.

Uneven wear may indicate alignment or suspension issues.

  • Maintain correct tire pressure
  • Rotate tires regularly
  • Check for cracks or bulges
  • Replace tires with low tread
  • Use seasonal tires if needed
Tread test: Insert coin → Depth should cover symbol
Battery Checks

Your car battery powers the starter, lights, and electronics.

Most batteries last 3–5 years depending on climate and usage.

Corroded terminals or slow starts may signal battery issues.

Voltage test: 12.6V when fully charged

Clean terminals with baking soda and water to prevent corrosion.

Use a multimeter to check battery health.

  • Inspect terminals monthly
  • Secure battery in place
  • Turn off lights when parked
  • Replace battery before rainy season if weak
  • Use jumper cables safely
Multimeter: Red to positive, black to negative → Read voltage
Brake Inspection

Brakes are critical for safety and should be inspected regularly.

Listen for squealing or grinding noises when braking.

Brake pads typically last 48,000 to 112,000 kilometers.

Warning sign: Brake pedal feels soft or spongy

Check brake fluid level and color—it should be clear or light yellow.

Have brakes professionally inspected during tire rotations.

  • Replace worn brake pads
  • Flush brake fluid every 2 years
  • Inspect rotors for grooves
  • Test emergency brake function
  • Avoid hard braking when possible
Brake fluid: Reservoir near engine → Check level and clarity
Dashboard Warnings

Dashboard lights alert you to potential issues with your vehicle.

Common lights include check engine, oil pressure, battery, and tire pressure.

Don’t ignore warning lights—some indicate urgent problems.

Check Engine: May signal emissions or sensor issues

Use an OBD-II scanner to read diagnostic trouble codes.

Refer to your owner’s manual for light meanings.

  • Check Engine = diagnostics needed
  • Oil Light = low pressure or level
  • Battery Light = charging issue
  • TPMS Light = tire pressure problem
  • ABS Light = brake system alert
OBD-II: Plug into port → Scan → Read code → Research fix
